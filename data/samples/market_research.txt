AI INFRASTRUCTURE MARKET RESEARCH — Q1 2025
Source: Synthesis Research Partners

=== MARKET OVERVIEW ===

The global AI infrastructure market reached $38B in 2024 and is projected to grow
at a 38% CAGR to $250B by 2028. Key demand drivers include:

1. Hyperscaler capex: AWS, Azure, and GCP combined AI infrastructure spend grew
   240% YoY in 2024, with $150B+ committed for 2025.
2. Enterprise AI adoption: 67% of Fortune 500 companies now have active AI
   initiatives, up from 31% in 2022.
3. Model complexity: Average LLM parameter count grew 10x between 2022 and 2024,
   increasing demand for optimisation services.

=== COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE ===

Direct competitors to AI infrastructure optimisation players:
- Large hyperscalers (AWS SageMaker, Azure ML): broad but not specialised
- NVIDIA DGX Cloud: hardware-first, high cost
- Scale AI: data-centric, limited model optimisation
- Modal Labs: developer-focused, smaller enterprise footprint

Differentiation factors that command premium valuation (EV/Revenue multiples):
- Proprietary dataset or model IP:     8x-14x EV/Revenue
- Strong NRR (>120%):                  8x-12x EV/Revenue
- Commodity tooling:                   3x-6x EV/Revenue

=== MACRO & RATE ENVIRONMENT ===

Federal Funds Rate (Q1 2025): 4.5%
Impact on AI valuations: Multiple compression of 15-25% vs 2021 peak.
EV/Forward Revenue for AI SaaS (public comparables): median 9.2x, range 5x-18x.

Interest rate sensitivity: Each 100bps rate increase historically compresses
AI SaaS EV/Revenue multiples by 3-5%.

=== RISK FACTORS IN SECTOR ===

1. Regulatory: EU AI Act enforcement begins mid-2025. Models above 10^25 FLOPs
   require registration and compliance audits. Timeline: 6-18 months.
2. Commoditisation: Open-source models (LLaMA, Mistral) reducing barriers to entry.
3. Key-person risk: Talent concentration in <500 AI researchers globally.
4. Customer concentration: Median enterprise AI vendor has 3-4 customers
   representing 50-70% of ARR at Series B stage.
